### You know where and why earthquakes are most likely to occur in Switzerland

By taking historical data it was possible to find patterns and to analyze which regions were more endangered of seismic activity. Valais was and therefore still is considered to be the most hazardous place in Switzerland closly followed by Basel and West Graubünden and St. Gallen.

In the Alps region the earthquakes are produced by compression of the African and Euroasian tectnoic plate which created and still forms the Alps.

In the Rhein region the earthquakes are triggered by technic faults in a diverging system, therefore a system where tehnic plates are pulled apart.

### You can explain the terms “hazard” and “risk” in the context of earthquakes

- The term `hazard` is very easily comprehended because it expresses solely the probability and intensity of earthquakes occurring in a specific geographic domain.
- The term `risk` describes a complex and intertwined system of variables which together express the risk. The fundamental component of risk is the hazard of an earthquake occurring in a specific place. To calculate the possible damage which such an earthquake could carry with it one needs to incorporate other factors as well like: density of population, site factors and protection of infrastructure. The damage and the hazard of an earthquake occurring in a geographic domain is called the risk. For example Graubünden and Basel have roughly the same hazard of earthquakes but an earthquake in the very loosely populated alps carries a much smaller damage with it then in Basel. Therefore the risk of earthquakes is much higher in the densely populated Basel.

## History of Geology

### You can explain how geology developed as a science and how it works in general

Geology is a very old scientific discipline which started to be shaped in ancient Greece. First historians like **Herodot** in 450 BC discovered a trivial fact (in modern times) that the earth surface is not immutable. He discovered this by observing the Nile and the Delta which it formed by rocks being transported by the river.

After establishing the fact that the earth is mutable again after the dark ages of the medieval times a new debate was raised of how rock was formed. Scientist like `Hutton` believed in **Plutonism** therefore that rock was formed from the internal temperature of the earth by magma rising to the surface and cooling off to form rock types like granite and basalt.

Plutonism turned out to be the correct theory supported by scientific observations but it was questioned by **Neptunism** a theory supported by `Werner`. It stated that all rocks on earth are sediments of an very old ocean that formed when the level of the ocean sank slowly over the ages. Neptunism would therefore need that the earths history was very short compared to Plutonism which could explain **deep time**. It also was able to explain how granite should have crystallized as a sediment. 

Scientist like `Smith` discovered that a fact that proved the theory of **deep time** together with Darwin's theory of evolution. He discovered that in specific layers of rock one can find the same fossils. Therefore one could take rock layers from two completely different regions and tell if there were from the same time by observing the fossils preserved in them. As evolution is a very slow process one can conclude from this observation that therefor the **layering** of the rock hand to take a long time.

Today we explain geological process using immutable process using the theory of **Uniformitarianism** which was developed by `Lyell`. We therefore take a few geological process as immutable which we also see today and try to explain past geological events using them.

## Geological Processes

- The process of `erosion` takes place when rock is slowly warn away in any manner. This includes aeolian abrasion by sand particles picked up by wind eroding other rocks into spheres. Frost wedging where water travels into cracks and expands when it freezes therefore crushing the rock apart. Glaciers and rivers erode massive amount of material as well.
- The process of `transportation` takes rock from one places and transports them by means of water like rivers to another place.
- The process of `Deposition` is the direct consequence of transportation and occurs at the place where transported sediments and rocks are stopped and pile up on each other. Examples include, deltas, lake basins, deposition of planctonic organisms by sedimentation, evaporation or direct deposiiton of lava.
- The process of `Lithification` can happen when rocks and sediment is depositet and they are binded into a solid stone structure.
- The `process` of folding can happen at a specific pressure and a specific temperature where rock becoms elastic and it shape can be changed very slowly.
- The process of `intrusion` takes place when magma from the core is pushed up into another layer.



Landscapes are shaped by mainly three geological processes or laws which are considered to me immutable

- Principle of original Horizontality which says that every layer that has come from sedimentation or deposition was created horizontally and if it has turned later.
- The principle of superposition says that platess which are nearer the surface are older then the ones nearer the core.
- The principle of Cross Cutting predicts that if a geological feautre cuts trough another the one cutting trough the other is the younger.
