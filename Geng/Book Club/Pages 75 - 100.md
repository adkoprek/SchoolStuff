# Pages 75–100

- Proles are the lowest class in the *1984* society.

- They are supposed to show the uninvolvement in politics and to symbolize ignorance.

- History can be rewritten, and the situation before the Party was gradually erased and changed to complement the Party.

- The telescreens can pick up slight changes like the frequency of a person’s pulse.

- Jones, Rutherford, and Aronson are former Party leaders during the revolution and were arrested for conspiring with Eurasia.

- They confessed to crimes that they did not commit, which Winston found out because of a picture of these three men taken at the same time as the crime was said to have happened.

- It is unusual for government workers to walk around the prole part of town, not to mention entering their pub.

- Winston was walking around in the prole part of town where a missile strike took place, which shows that war is still happening.

- Winston entered a prole pub where he asked an old man about life before the Party.

- After not receiving any valuable information, Winston left the pub, walked around town, and stumbled into the antiquity store.

- Winston is fascinated by the stuff in the store and even buys something.

- The owner of the shop took him upstairs, where Winston noticed the absence of the telescreen.
