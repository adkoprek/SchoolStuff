## Symbols

### West & East Egg / old vs new money

- West egg is associated with a wasteful and public life style and people which gained a lot of many recently (new money) without any social status.
- East egg is in contrary connected to people with year long influence which do not have the need to show their money.

### Green Light

The green light stands for Gatsby's dream which he urned to achieve but never reaches. It functions as a critique for the American dream which is in reality associated with similar disappointment.

Nick thinks of the light as the dream of explorers to reach America and of other people having dreams and being pushed back by the sea into the past.

### NYC

Shows the fast life style in the roaring twenties, with people form all classes and no regulations in place. Reckless driving, Illegal consumption of alcohol, inequality and violence were just the beginning of the dark side.

### Gatsby Parties with the Oranges and Lemmons

Show the wastefulness of the wealthy class in the roaring twenties.

### Human Stratification

Describes the classes in a society people were pushed into based on family, wealth and income. As in the example of Gatsby these barriers were not easily penetrable. 

### Passive Voice

Passive Voice was used in the text when speaking about something bad like violence, death or crime. Nick and others used this voice to distance themselves from these events, denying any accountability and trying to to feel any more grief after the traumatizing years of the war. 

### Social Image

The social image or reputation was of high importance mainly for women. Who if they hoped to get married needed to be innocent and pure. This was still an very important concept in the roaring twenties and can be observed in Daisys behavior, nearly instinct, to preserve her image.

### The Valley of Ashes

Is piece of land between East Egg and NYC which was created by dumping of industrial ashes. It shows the results or byproduct of the wealthy class of the society only pursuing pleasure.

### The Eyes of Doctor T. J. Eckleburg

They might represent someone like God watching and judging over the American society and its wasteland. As they eyes were also in a bad state and falling apart they might be also be interpreted as a connection to the promised American dream.

### The Automobile

Cars represent freedom as they allowed people to move quickly and effortlessly anywhere and anytime they wanted which is an important factor why the roaring twenties were considered to be so fast.

On the other hand in almost every chapter cars are also associated with destruction and danger. As there were no regulations in place for cars yet and people drove cars when they were drunk.

### Shirts

Daisy cries because of Gatsby's shirts for no apparent reason at first glance. But more likely the shirts showed Daisy how Gatsby - or rather his position - changed over the years and he would be able to take care of her now. But this still was a false sense of security as Gatsby was not in the same class as Daisy.

## Characters

### Nick Carrraway

Is the main 1st person narrator of the novel and describes everything from his perspective or how he was told by other people. Nick comes from a wealthy familly in the Midwest and moved to Long Island to start a life on his own after the war. He thinks of himself as morally superior to most other people.

### Daisy

Daisy is Nicks cousin and Tom's wife and is described a foolish, beautiful and dependent woman. She comes from old money and is used to a certain custom of life, as "her voice is full of money". She tends to not take any risk and to take the path which is the safest for her reputation.

### Tom Buchanan

Tom Buchanan is the husband of Daisy and comes from old money. He was educated at Yale and is a big, loud, powerful and strong man. He has a short temper and strong opinions which Nick does not agree with. He is the antagonist of the story.

### Jordan Baker

Is represents the "new woman" in the roaring twenties and the slight emancipation it brought with it. She dates Nick. 

### Jay Gatsby

He originated as James Gatz rose from an impoverished childhood in rural North Dakota. He is obsessed with Daisy which who he has fallen in love before the war and now has moved to West Egg to be closer to her. He hopes that Daisy would show up at one of his parties. He always wanted to belong the high class of old money. To achieve this he earned money trough questionable sources and became extremely rich. To separate himself from his past, he reinvented himself as Jay Gatsby with elaborate stories and lies. To always stay in his personae he does not drink at his parties to always be able to manipulate people to believe in his new self.

### Myrtle

Myrtle is Tom's mistress and is the wife of Wilson and in comparison to Daisy she is not adored and respected but is sexualized by Tom.

### Wilson

Wilson is Myrtle's husband and represents the population of the valley of ashes and the type of person which suffered in the roaring twenties from the overconsumption of the wealth class.

### Meyer Wolfsheim

He is a friend of Gatsby with which Gatsby run mysterious business. In the end he did not even show up to Gatsby's funeral./

## Historical Context

### World War 1

Took place between 1914 to 1918 so four years before the start of the novel. Many Americans fought in this war and died for a cause that they did not understand. The ones that have returned were often traumatized and wanted to indulge themselves in activities to forget about the mental and physical effects the war had on them.

### Roaring 20s

Refers to the 1920s decade where the American economy prospered after the war. As a result some people got extremely wealthy and started to close the gap to the old money. But many people - like the ones in the valley of ashes - had to suffer the consequences of this growth. 

### Prohibition

The prohibition was a time period between 1920 and 1933 where it was forbidden to produce and sell alcoholic beverages. This lead to alcohol begin sold illegally by bootleggers. This alcohol often was of poor quality and was uncontrolled leading to a lot of violence.
