# Speech

**Democracy — we live it, and we love it. But do we still *live* it?**  
Today we live as if democracy is something obvious and eternal. But it is not. It is our *duty* to keep democracy alive and to pass it on to new generations.

You’ve probably noticed, as I have, in the news lately, that democracy seems to be slowly undermined — by heads of state like Donald Trump and his *self-appointed minister of efficiency*, Elon Musk. Why is this happening now?

We live — or rather, *used to live* — in a world where there were two global forces of domination. The first was raw military power, the second was economic power.
These two forces used to determine who controlled the world. We put our trust in governmental institutions run by serious politicians and safeguarded through democracy — with checks and balances and the involvement of many people in parliaments.

In the past, power was physical and marched on battlefields. But today, it is stored in computers. A new form of domination has joined the race — one not measured by money or tanks built.  
It is the *empire of data* — a force controlled by gigantic private companies capable of finding out *everything* about you.

We all use WhatsApp, Instagram, Google, YouTube, TikTok, ChatGPT were we spend a majority of our online activity. These platforms are run by just four companies: Meta, Alphabet, ByteDance, and OpenAI.  
Every message, every post, every like gives this digital empire more access to your **mind** — and with that, more power.

Power means control.  
But this new control is bound by neither constitutions nor democracy.

The massive amount of data that we consume on these platforms tells more about you than you think. By sharing your data so publicly, you give these companies enormous power. They can analyze what we consume and subtly influence what we see — pushing us into bubbles, polarizing our opinions.  
Yet all this psychological manipulation makes us *believe* these are our choices.  
But in reality, it’s more like a puppet show.

And as we all know — with great power comes great responsibility.

These companies are run by technocrats who see you not as a person, but as a number in their statistics — not as a human being with opinions, but as a datapoint to be optimized.  
In their eyes, you can be manipulated to control your opinions, your desires, and your morals.  
And those three — opinions, desires, morals — form the foundation of our political views.  
In this way, technocrats gain more political control.

But at what cost is this happening?  
Are we willing to sacrifice privacy for convenience, freedom of speech for comfort, and conversations for clicks?  
And most importantly — do we trust that the few people running these companies will act responsibly?

Already today, algorithms developed by technocratic institutions control large portions of human life — whether you can get a loan, what insurance you can buy, and what you read or watch first.  
It may seem harmless now, but it will only become riskier with the rise of AI.

The question we must now ask is: **what kind of future do we want instead of this mess?**

Democracy is a story — a story invented long ago for the benefit of all humans.  
And this story lives only in our minds and our books.  
If we stop reminding ourselves of this story, it will be forgotten — buried beneath a flood of algorithms.  
We must not let that happen.  
We must remind each other, from time to time, of the great privilege of living in a democracy.

The most important thing in the coming years is that we do **not** give a handful of companies access to practically all our information.  
Because information is power.  
Power is control.  
And control that is not bound by democracy.

We have to stand up for ourselves and not allow a few people to have absolute control over our views and beliefs — using technology that isn’t even fifty years old.

Which brings me to my final conclusion:  
**Do not let data manipulate you — and democracy will not be replaced by technocracy.**
