# Stories

- **1984** by George Orwell is often regarded as one of the greatest dystopian novels. It explores themes such as Big Brother, propaganda, and the manipulation of truth by a totalitarian regime.

- **To Kill a Mockingbird** by Harper Lee tells the story of a Black man who is wrongly accused of raping a white woman. The novel addresses themes of justice, racial prejudice, class inequality, and segregation.

- **Brave New World** by Aldous Huxley depicts a future society where people are engineered biologically and conditioned psychologically. It questions the cost of comfort and stability when individuality and freedom are sacrificed.
